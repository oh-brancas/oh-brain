02

Daily Flow

Your Tier 1 daily rhythm. Five phases from wake-up to shutdown, with built-in responses for interruptions, stress, and energy crashes.

Morning
Sunlight within 30 min of waking
Brain dump everything to your single capture list
Identify Top 3 priorities for today
Focus Block
90 min on your #1 priority
No messages, no meetings
If interrupted: "I'll get to this at [time]"
Collaboration
Meetings, reviews, team work
New requests: "Yes. What should I deprioritize?"
Batch messages and replies
Process
Clarify and organize inbox
Stress spike? Sigh x3
Energy crash? 10-min NSDR
Shutdown
Review open tasks
Write tomorrow's Top 3
Close all apps. "Shutdown complete."

04

Under Pressure

When the team says "everything is urgent" and your stress spikes. Four scenarios, four responses.

"Everything is urgent"
Ask: "Which of these matters most by Friday?" Force a ranking. If they can't rank it, it's not actually urgent.
"Can you do this too?"
"Yes, I can do that. What should I deprioritize?" Every yes is a no to something else. Make the trade-off visible.
"I can't think straight"
Physiological sigh x3. Then brain-dump everything to your list. Just get it out of your head. Pick ONE thing. Do that.
"I'm running on empty"
10-min NSDR. Close eyes, body scan, do nothing. Not a luxury — a reset. You'll get more done in the next hour than grinding through fog.

Your first five moves

No setup required. Do these on Monday and see what changes.

Capture everything
One list. Paper, phone, whatever. Every task, idea, request goes there. Not your head.
2
Physiological sigh

3
Forced trade-off

4
Shutdown ritual
5 min before closing laptop: review tasks, write tomorrow's top 3, close everything.
5
Friday retrospective
15 min on Friday: where did your time actually go this week? Classify by quadrant.
